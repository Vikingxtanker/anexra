Since ANEXRA is positioning itself as a premium healthcare education platform for Pharm.D, B.Pharm, and D.Pharm students (not just a simple course marketplace), I would structure the page more like a modern SaaS learning platform rather than a typical Udemy clone.

## Recommended Page Structure

### 1. CoursesHero.tsx

**Goal:** Explain why these courses matter.

**Layout**

* Large headline
* Short description (6–10 lines)
* CTA buttons
* Course statistics

Example:

> Learn Industry-Relevant Pharmacy & Clinical Skills
> Advance your career with expert-led certificate programs designed for Pharm.D, B.Pharm, and D.Pharm students. Build practical clinical knowledge, strengthen patient-care skills, earn certificates, and enhance your CV with job-ready competencies. Every course includes structured learning modules, assessments, and completion certification.

**Buttons**

* Explore Courses
* View Certifications

**Stats Row**

* 5000+ Students
* 20+ Courses
* Industry Certificates
* Self-Paced Learning

---

### 2. CoursesList.tsx

Fetch from Supabase.

#### Course Card Design

Each card should contain:

* Course Thumbnail
* Course Category
* Course Title
* Instructor
* Duration
* Modules Count
* Certificate Badge
* Difficulty Level
* Rating
* Enroll Button

Example:

```
Clinical Patient Counselling
Beginner

✓ Certificate Included
✓ 12 Modules
✓ 6 Hours Content
✓ Self Paced

[Enroll Now]
```

# Premium SaaS Layout Order

```txt
CoursesHero
↓
FeaturedCourse
↓
CoursesList
↓
LearningPath
↓
CoursesImportance
↓
CertificatePreview
↓
CareerOutcomes
↓
Testimonials
↓
FAQ
↓
CoursesCTA
```

This flow converts much better because it follows:

**Attention → Trust → Courses → Benefits → Proof → Action**

which is exactly how platforms like Coursera, Udemy, and LinkedIn Learning structure their course pages.
